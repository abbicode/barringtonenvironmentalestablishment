As members of our community, we have a responsibility to protect our planet. We’re a student-led organization that aims to BEE the change.

Climate Change

We’re in the middle of a climate crisis. Climate change is one of the greatest challenges our generation will face -- but we have to win the fight. According to the United Nations, “Increased temperatures [due to climate change] have a domino effect on the whole globe leading to intense droughts, water scarcity, severe fires, rising sea levels, flooding, melting polar ice, catastrophic storms and declining biodiversity.

How Climate Change Affects Rhode Island

Climate change, if left unchecked, will have devastating local impacts. The Rhode Island State Government stated, “Climate change affects the whole world, including Rhode Island. Rhode Island will experience warmer air and water temperatures, more extreme weather events such as droughts, intense precipitation, severe storms and flooding, rising sea levels, and shorter winters and longer summers. Climate change risks Rhode Island’s natural environment, health, welfare, economic well-being, and more.

What Towns Can do About Climate Change

Local change can have a huge impact on the greenhouse emissions and environmental health of the community. Individual community actions such as advocating for the climate, supporting local farms, reducing energy use, and creating green space can really help the climate crisis. That's why the BEE aims to make local change.
